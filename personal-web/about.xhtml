Investigaçom

A finalidade da minha tese é a elaboraçom dum estudo que analize rigorosamente, servindo-se dumha metodologia filológica, a variaçom lingüística que se documenta nos três grandes códices que enviam a lírica medieval galego-portuguesa: o Cancioneiro da Ajuda (A), o Cancioneiro da Biblioteca Nacional de Lisboa (B) e o Cancioneiro da Biblioteca Vaticana (V), assim como nos fragmentos menores (o Pergaminho Vindel (N), o Pergaminho Sharrer (T), a folha de Madrid (M) e as folhas do Porto (P)).

Este projeto marca-se como objetivo a preparaçom dumha ediçom digital mui concreta, que permita obter um melhor conhecimento da transmissom manuscrita da lírica galego-portuguesa. Para isso, depois dumha collatio informatizada em que se etiquetam todos os fenómenos de variaçom textual, procederá-se à extraçom de aqueles dados que se considerarem relevantes; poderemos, assim, organizar a informaçom disponível e proceder ao estudo da variaçom en todas as suas vertentes, prestando umha maior atençom às de corte lingüístico. Para mais informaçom visita a seçom de Estudos Lingüísticos.

About me

My name is Helena Bermúdez Sabel and I am a PhD candidate at the University of Santiago de Compostela. This website is my attempt to get together different materials I have been designing regarding the Galician-Portuguese poetic school.

On one hand, this site will contain linguistic data that I am gathering as part of my dissertation. This project, entitled Galician-Portuguese secular lyrics: Philology and Historical Linguistics is supervised by the professors Pilar Lorenzo Gradín and Mariña Arbor Aldea.

On the other hand, I am interested in the sociohistorical context in which this cultural movement was born. I am concerned with the dynamics of power in society, the diverse ways in which power is transferred and especially, the role of culture in maintaing the social order. As a result, I intend to analyze the relatioships between cultural producers and centers of power through the plotting of social networks and geospatial maps.

Research

I should talk about my dissertation here.

My dissertation project is concerned with textual variation in the manuscript transmission of Galician-Portuguese medieval poetry through the development of an interlinear edition. After compiling a corpus based on diachronic criteria and stemmatic filiation, I am marking it up following the TEI's Parallel Segmentation Method.

I identify all textual differences. Thus, all the copy errors, linguistic variants, equipollent readings and the graphematic variants have been categorized according to the Feature Structures module. This markup allows us to create a no-SQL database with systematic exploration, hence providing valuable information about the copyists’ origin and the scriptoria involved, as well as about the identification of the filiations between the sources.
